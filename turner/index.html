01 · The Opportunity

The stadium will speak for itself. The impact needs a storyteller.

Herbert, Tony, thank you for the conversation. It was clear within minutes that this project already has its story. It just has not been told on screen yet.

When the new stadium opens, every camera in the country will point at the building. What they will not see is what happened around it for three years. A training camp that turned neighbors into craft professionals. Small businesses that grew up inside a landmark project. Students who stood on the site and saw a future. That is the story Turner and the Titans get to own, and it deserves the same standard of production as the building itself.

Our goal is simple. When a Turner or Titans executive steps up to speak, the film that plays before them should do the heavy lifting. And anyone who finds these stories afterward should understand exactly what this project meant to Nashville.

Pillar I · People

The TC2 training camp

Cohorts of Middle Tennessee residents trained across the trades and graduating into construction careers, with three cohorts still ahead of us to capture.

Pillar II · Business

Small business assistance

Nearly one hundred small, women owned, and minority owned firms carried real scopes on a landmark build. Their growth is the project's quietest headline.

Pillar III · Community

Outreach across the region

Students, universities, high schools, and neighborhood programs brought onto the site and into the industry through years of engagement.

05 · What We Deliver

The work, in concrete terms.

Four phases take this from ideation to launch.

01

Story mapping and pre production

We sit down with your team, and with Johari and the Titans at the right moment, to map the story before anything is recorded. Who speaks, what each voice carries, and how the pieces fit. Tony's interview list becomes a locked roster, and we request archival footage from the Titans library so the edit starts with the full history in hand.

02

Interview and site production

Multi camera interview setups with cinema lighting and broadcast grade audio, in the spaces Turner designates on site or in conference settings. We build the annual meeting week into a concentrated interview block while the national community team is in Nashville, and we capture cohorts 13 through 15 in the trailers while the trailers are still there.

03

Edit, archive weave, and story shaping

Every film gets its own arc. New interviews are woven with archival groundbreaking and graduation footage so the story spans the full life of the project. Color grade, audio mix, and clean titles in a look that respects both the Turner and Titans brands. Your team reviews online and we refine through revision rounds.

04

Delivery for every screen and stage

Masters arrive ready for the premiere screen, the boardroom, the website, and social channels. Files come through a shared folder with clear naming, organized as a catalog your teams can pull from for years. We archive the masters for twelve months after delivery.
